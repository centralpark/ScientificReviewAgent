Recent studies presented at the American Association for Cancer Research (AACR) annual meetings over the past three years highlight significant advancements in the treatment and interception of pre-cancerous (preneoplastic) lesions. Successfully treating these lesions is a critical strategy in oncology; population modeling indicates that intercepting preneoplastic lesions before they progress could theoretically prevent thousands of invasive cancer cases in the US annually, particularly for deadly malignancies like pancreatic and stomach cancers [1].   \n  \n**Skin Pre-Cancers (Actinic Keratosis & SCC Precursors)**  \n*   **Enhanced Photodynamic Therapy (PDT):** PDT is a standard treatment for actinic keratosis and precursors to squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), though its efficacy can vary. An *in vivo* pilot study using murine models demonstrated that enhancing standard PDT cream (20% methyl aminolevulinate) with 3% epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) significantly boosted treatment efficacy. This combination reduced skin lesion volumes by up to 50% more than conventional PDT alone [2].  \n*   **Epigenetic Targeting:** Chronic sun exposure drives actinic keratosis via the activation of the epigenetic modifier BRD4. Researchers found that a novel RXR agonist, 9cUAB30, effectively blocks this BRD4 activation. In murine models, oral administration of 9cUAB30 successfully abrogated UVB-induced skin inflammation and keratinocyte hyper-proliferation, showing strong potential to treat photo-damaged pre-malignant lesions and halt their progression to invasive SCC [3].  \n  \n**Cervical Pre-Cancers (CIN2/3)**  \n*   **Topical 5-Fluorouracil (5-FU):** Treating cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN2/3) is particularly challenging in high-risk populations, such as women living with HIV, due to high recurrence rates. Research presented on global health initiatives highlighted the clinical efficacy of self-administered intravaginal 5-FU. Trial data showed that using 5-FU reduced CIN2/3 recurrence to just 8% (compared to 31% with observation) and induced disease regression in 93% of patients when used as a primary treatment. Current studies are focused on validating the feasibility of this highly effective, scalable topical therapy in low- and middle-income countries [4].  \n  \n**Internal Pre-Cancers (Gastrointestinal & Gynecological)**  \n*   **Multi-Cancer Early Detection (MCED) and Surgery:** Blood-based MCED testing is proving effective not just for early cancer detection, but for identifying high-risk pre-malignant conditions. In the DETECT-A study, positive blood tests (detecting *PIK3CA*, *TP53*, or *KRAS* mutations) identified asymptomatic individuals with clinically significant pre-cancerous conditions. These included an ovarian mucinous cystadenoma, an appendiceal mucinous neoplasm, and colonic polyps with high-grade dysplasia. Following targeted surgical excision of these pre-cancerous lesions, all affected participants remained completely cancer-free [5].  \n  \nReferences
